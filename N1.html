Calcium

Calcium is the most abundant mineral in the body and is mostly found
in bones and teeth.

Calcium interacts with phosphorus to create healthy bones and teeth,
and with magnesium to maintain a healthy heart and blood vessels.
20% of the calcium in adult bones is reabsorbed and replaced every
year.

Calcium efficacy:

  1. Maintain strong bones and healthy teeth
  2. Maintain a regular heart rhythm
  3. Relieve insomnia symptoms
  4. Help the metabolism of iron in the body
  5. Strengthen the nervous system, especially its stimulation
    transmission function

Calcium supplementation cycle:

It is recommended to start at a young age and supplement daily.

Manifestations of excess calcium:

Hypercalcemia.

Food origin:

Milk and dairy products, soybeans and all legumes, peanuts, cabbage
vegetables, green cauliflower, green leafy vegetables, walnuts,
sunflower seeds, etc.

Need crowd:

People who often suffer from low back pain and dysmenorrhea can
improve their symptoms by taking calcium supplements; teenagers
with neuralgia during growth period can reduce the pain by taking
more calcium; people with low blood intake and menopause should take
in more calcium; if you drink it regularly Carbonated drinks require
calcium supplementation. Because these drinks contain extremely high
phosphorus, they will consume calcium in the body and increase the
possibility of osteoporosis.

Deficiency disorder

Calcium deficiency can lead to conditions such as rickets, rickets,
and osteoporosis.
