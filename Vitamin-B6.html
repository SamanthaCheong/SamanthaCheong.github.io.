Vitamin B6 (pyridoxine)

Vitamin B6 is a water-soluble vitamin that is excreted from the body within
8 hours after digestion, so it needs to be supplemented with food or nutritional
supplements. In fact, vitamin B 6 is made up of several substances. It is a
necessary substance for making antibodies and red blood cells.

When eating high-protein foods, you should increase its intake. Because the
bacteria in the intestine have the ability to synthesize B 6, it is necessary
to eat more vegetables. In addition, B 6 is essential when digesting vitamin B
12 and when making hydrochloric acid and magnesium, B 6 is also necessary.

Vitamin B6 effects:

  1. Able to properly digest and absorb protein and fat
  2. Help convert tryptophan, an essential amino acid, into niacin
  3. Prevent various neurological and skin diseases
  4. Relieve vomiting
  5. Promote the synthesis of nucleic acids and prevent the aging of tissues
    and organs
  6. Reduce dry mouth and difficulty urinating caused by taking antidepressants
  7. Reduce nighttime muscle spasms, cramps and paralysis and other symptoms of
    hand and foot neuritis
  8. It is a natural diuretic.

Vitamin B6 supplement cycle:

Vitamin B 6 only stays in the human body for 8 hours, so it needs to be
supplemented every day.

Food origin:

Green leafy vegetables, beer, wheat bran, malt, liver, soybeans, cabbage,
brown rice, eggs, oats, peanuts, walnuts, etc.

Need crowd:

Anemia, seborrheic dermatitis, and tongue inflammation are all related to
lack of vitamin B 6; women taking birth control pills should increase their
intake; people who consume a lot of protein must take in more vitamin B 6.

Eficiency disorder:

Anemia, seborrheic dermatitis, glossitis.
