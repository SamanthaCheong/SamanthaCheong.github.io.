Vitamin E (tocopherol)

Vitamin E is a fat-soluble vitamin known as a blood vessel scavenger.
It has a significant effect in preventing cardiovascular disease and
has a strong antioxidant effect.

Not only can it prevent adult diseases, it also has the function of
delaying aging and maintaining youthfulness.

Vitamin E effects:

  1. Delay the oxidative aging of cells, moisturize the skin, eliminate
    spots, and maintain a youthful appearance.
  2. Purifies the blood, reduces the density of low-density lipoproteins
    in the blood, and prevents hardening of the arteries.
  3. Promote normal red blood cell production and prevent blood clotting
  4. Lowers blood pressure and reduces the chance of ischemic heart
    disease.
  5. Enhance the detoxification function of the liver, protect the body
    and reduce fatigue.
  6. Promote the secretion of sex hormones, improve reproductive capacity
    and prevent miscarriage.
  7. Recommended daily intake of vitamin E:
  8. Adults should take 10 to 12 mg daily. Pregnant and breastfeeding
    women should increase their dosage by 5 to 10 mg. Menopausal women
    need 20mg.

Vitamin E supplementation cycle:

1 to 2 times a day.

Symptoms of vitamin E overdose:

Abdominal pain, diarrhea, precocious puberty in children and adolescents,
breast enlargement, dizziness, and nausea

Food origin:

Soybeans, vegetable oils, malt, dried fruits, citrus, green leafy vegetables,
unrefined cereals, eggs, eels, squid, etc.

Need crowd:

People who drink tap water disinfected with chlorine must take more vitamin
E; people who take birth control pills and hormones must supplement vitamin E;
patients with cardiovascular disease, Parkinson's disease, pregnant women, and
middle-aged and elderly people must supplement vitamin E.

Deficiency disorder:

Hemolytic anemia, muscle degeneration, reproductive dysfunction, hair loss,
aging, etc.
