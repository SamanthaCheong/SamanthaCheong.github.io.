Niacin (vitamin B3, niacin)

Vitamin B3 is the one that the human body requires the most among the
B vitamins. It is not only a vitamin that maintains a healthy digestive
system, but is also an indispensable substance for the synthesis of sex
hormones.

For modern people whose lives are full of stress, the role of niacin in
maintaining the health of the nervous system and normal brain function
cannot be ignored.

Vitamin B3 effects:

  1. Promote the health of the digestive system and reduce gastrointestinal
    disorders
  2. Make the skin healthier
  3. Prevent and relieve severe migraines
  4. Reduce cholesterol and triglycerides, promote blood circulation, and
    lower blood pressure
  5. Reduce the uncomfortable symptoms of Meniere’s syndrome
  6. Enable the human body to fully utilize food to increase energy
  7. Treat inflammation of the mouth and lips and prevent bad breath
  8. Recommended daily intake of vitamin B3:
  9. The recommended daily intake for adults is 13~19mg. Pregnant women are
    20mg; lactating women are 22mg.

Food origin:

Whole wheat products, brown rice, mung beans, sesame, peanuts, mushrooms,
seaweed, figs, dairy products, eggs, chicken, liver, lean meat, fish, etc.

Need crowd:

People who have trouble with cholesterol will benefit from increasing their
intake of niacin; when the skin is particularly exposed to the sun's rays, it
is often an early symptom of niacin deficiency; people with dermatitis, peeling,
and rough skin need niacin; People who are deficient in vitamins B1 , B2, and
B6 need additional supplements because they cannot synthesize nicotinic acid from
tryptophan by themselves. People who are often nervous, restless, or even suffer
from schizophrenia are beneficial to supplementing vitamin B3; diabetics and those
with hyperthyroidism also need niacin.

Deficiency disorder:

Pellagra
