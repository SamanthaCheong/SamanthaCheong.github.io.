Vitamin C (ascorbic acid)

Vitamin C is a water-soluble vitamin and the most well-known nutrient.
It is not only a beauty elixir, but also an antioxidant, cell-protecting
and even effective anti-cancer vitamin.

It is commonly found in fruits and vegetables, but is easily destroyed by
changes in the external environment.

Vitamin C effects:

  1. Increase skin elasticity and prevent spots
  2. Promote wound healing, treat trauma, burns, gum bleeding, and
    accelerate recovery after surgery
  3. Prevent viral and bacterial infections and enhance immune system
    function
  4. Reduce cholesterol in the blood and reduce the chance of venous
    thrombosis
  5. It can treat common colds and has preventive effects
  6. Can weaken the effects of many substances that can cause allergies
  7. Help absorb iron, disintegrate folic acid, and prevent scurvy
  8. Helps prevent the formation of carcinogen nitrosamines and has
    anti-cancer effects
  9. It can make protein cells adhere to each other, help produce collagen,
    prevent aging and extend life
  10. Recommended daily intake of vitamin C:
  11. The recommended intake for adults is 60mg per day. More is needed during
    pregnancy and lactation, which should be 70~95mg. But be aware that although
    vitamin C is good, excessive intake may cause stones or side effects. In addition,
    do not take vitamin C preparations or eat foods rich in vitamin C within 3 hours
    after taking ginseng.

Vitamin C supplementation cycle:

Vitamin C only stays in the human body for 4 hours, so supplement it at
least twice a day.

Food origin:

Green vegetables, green peppers, tomatoes, peppers, cauliflower, potatoes,
apricots, apples, peaches, etc.

Need crowd:

People who smoke, drink alcohol, and love to eat meat can benefit from supplementing
with vitamin C; people who take birth control pills, antibiotics, and aspirin should
increase their intake of vitamin C; carbon monoxide can destroy vitamin C, so people
who live in cities should increase their intake of vitamin C. Ingestion.

Deficiency disorder:

Severe lack of vitamin C can cause scurvy.
