Vitamin B5 (pantothenic acid)

Vitamin B5 has the function of producing antibodies and also plays an
important role in maintaining hair, skin and blood health.

Almost all foods contain pantothenic acid, so deficiency is generally
nothing to worry about.

Vitamin B5 effects:

  1. Create and renew body tissues
  2. Help wound healing
  3. Produce antibodies to resist infectious diseases
  4. Prevent fatigue and help resist stress
  5. Alleviate the side effects and toxins of various antibiotics
  6. Relieve premenstrual syndrome
  7. Alleviate nausea symptoms

Recommended daily intake of vitamin B5:

The recommended intake for adults is 10mg.

Food origin:

Green leafy vegetables, unrefined grains, corn, peas, peanuts,
nuts, honey, lean meat, animal offal, etc.

Need crowd:

Pantothenic acid is needed by people who often experience tingling
in their hands and feet. Taking pantothenic acid can provide resistance
to upcoming and existing tensions. It should be noted for those suffering
from allergies, arthritis patients, those taking antibiotics and women taking
birth control pills. Supplement pantothenic acid.

Deficiency disorder:

Hypoglycemia, duodenal ulcers, blood and skin abnormalities are all symptoms
of pantothenic acid deficiency.
