Iron

Iron is the main substance for maintaining life, the main substance for
manufacturing heme and myoglobin, and a necessary substance for promoting
the metabolism of vitamin B family. Iron and calcium are the two most
lacking nutrients in the diet of Chinese people, especially women.

Only about 8% of the iron ingested by the body is actually absorbed into
the bloodstream. Most of the iron in the body is used to make heme. Heme
is recycled and reused when blood cells replace new cells every 120 days.
Protein-bound iron is stored in the body, while tissue iron (present in
myoglobulin) is stored in very small amounts in the body.

Iron efficacy:

  1. Promote development
  2. Increase resistance to disease
  3. Regulate tissue breathing and prevent fatigue
  4. Constitute heme to prevent and treat anemia caused by iron deficiency
  5. Restore good skin color.

Iron supplementation cycle:

Daily supplementation is recommended.

Symptoms of iron overdose:

Vomiting, heart failure, sexual dysfunction.

Food origin:

Liver, peaches, lean meat, shellfish, nuts, asparagus, spinach, oats,
honey, beans, etc.

Need crowd:

Women, especially pregnant women, need to supplement iron, but be aware
that taking too much iron in pregnant women can cause iron poisoning in
the fetus; If you take anti-inflammatories or take a daily aspirin, you
may need an iron supplement. People who regularly drink black tea or coffee
should be aware that drinking large amounts of black tea and coffee can
hinder iron absorption.

Deficiency disorder

Iron deficiency can lead to iron deficiency anemia.
