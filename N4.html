Potassium

Potassium is a silver-white soft metal, waxy, can be cut with a knife, has
a low melting and boiling point, is less dense than water, and is extremely
chemically active (more active than sodium). It is one of the elements of group
IA and belongs to the alkali metal. Potassium does not exist in elemental form
in nature.Potassium is widely distributed on land and ocean in the form of salt.
Potassium is also one of the important components in human muscle tissue and nerve
tissue.

Potassium and sodium work together to maintain the balance of water in the body and
normal heart rhythm (potassium works within cells, sodium only works outside cells);
when the balance of potassium and sodium is out of balance, it can damage the
function of nerves and muscles.

Effects of potassium:

  1. It can help transport oxygen to the brain and improve clarity of thinking
  2. Help the body process waste
  3. Lower blood pressure
  4. Helps in the treatment of allergies

Potassium supplementation cycle:

Daily replenishment

Symptoms of excess potassium:

Difficulty excreting, muscle weakness, slow heartbeat, and muscle twitching.

Food origin:

Citrus fruits, bananas, cantaloupes, tomatoes, celery and other green leafy vegetables,
sunflower seeds, potatoes, etc.

Need crowd:

People who drink a lot of coffee, alcohol and eat sweets are more likely to feel
fatigued, which is caused by potassium deficiency; People with severe diarrhea are
more likely to lose potassium even when they are in a state of urinary retention.
If diuretics are used, more potassium will be lost; If you don't eat staple food
(carbohydrates) to lose weight, you will not only lose weight, but the potassium
content in your body will also decrease. This results in weakened physical strength
and slower reactions; Nervous and physical stress can lead to potassium deficiency.

Deficiency disorder

Hypoglycemia, edema and potassium deficiency are closely related.
